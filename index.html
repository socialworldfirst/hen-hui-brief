The brand

WorldFirst is a cross-border payments specialist owned by Ant Group. We help small and medium business owners move money between countries — paying suppliers, receiving marketplace payouts, holding multi-currency accounts. We are not a bank. We are the rails between banks that don't talk to each other smoothly.

For this batch, the product hero is: direct CNY rails to Chinese suppliers, same-day, near mid-market exchange rates. That is the proof point every script lands on.

02

The audience

SEA-based SME operators sourcing from China. Priority markets: Thailand and Malaysia. Secondary: Vietnam, Philippines, Indonesia, Singapore.

They have small teams. They source on 1688 or Alibaba. They pay suppliers in wires or PayPal or credit cards. They know FX margin exists but cannot quantify it. They've had a wire delayed. They've been told no to Visa by a supplier and didn't understand why. They are not finance people. Speak plain.

They use English at a 7th-8th grade reading level on average. No jargon without a quick definition. No acronyms without a spell-out.

03

The voice

We voice. Official WorldFirst account. Not first-person "I". Not "you guys".

Direct, modern, practical. Like a friend who imports, not a bank.

Value first, viral second, brand mentions last. Teach something concrete in every script. The brand earns the CTA at the end.

Numbers, not adjectives. "Saves money" is weak. "1,000 dollars per wire on a 50K transfer" is strong.

No em-dashes (the long horizontal line —). Use commas, colons, periods, line breaks.

1. My bank says sent. My supplier says nothing. Both right.

Story P1-A9-1 · PH2 Final VO 60 seconds 147 words
Final VO (read this verbatim)

Is it possible to lose a container in five days without losing the money? Your bank app shows sent. Your supplier's WhatsApp shows nothing. You haven't lost the money. You've lost the day. Then the week. Then the order. Because your supplier won't load raw materials until the CNY hits their account. So every hour the wire sits at an intermediary bank, your delivery date slips. Three intermediaries, two days of conversion windows, one AML hold above a threshold nobody told you about, plus a Friday public holiday in Shenzhen. That's a five-day silence. On a Q4 order, five days is your container. On a launch SKU, five days is your competitor beating you to the shelf. The wire isn't lost. The visibility is. WorldFirst removes the correspondent chain entirely. CNY straight to Chinese bank accounts, same-day, with a tracking reference your supplier can match. Search WorldFirst.

Angle

The cost of a wire isn't the money. It's the time. A delayed wire = a delayed container = a missed Q4 = a competitor on the shelf.

Why the hook works

Opens with a paradox — "lose a container without losing the money." Curiosity gap. Forces the viewer to keep watching to resolve the contradiction.

Story beats (in order)
  1. Paradox (sent on the app vs nothing in supplier's WhatsApp)
  2. Escalation (lost the day → the week → the order)
  3. Mechanism (supplier won't load raw materials until CNY hits)
  4. Compounding cost (3 intermediaries + AML + Friday holiday = 5 days)
  5. Stakes (Q4 container, launch SKU, competitor beats you to shelf)
  6. Reframe ("the wire isn't lost. The visibility is.")
  7. WF positioning (direct rail, same-day, tracking reference)
Numbers to nail (deliver exactly)

Three intermediaries · two days of conversion windows · one AML hold · Friday public holiday in Shenzhen · five-day silence

Tone & pacing

Urgent but controlled. Operator-to-operator. Slight tension build. Not lecturing.

Visual / B-roll ideas (director's call, these are starting points)
  • Talking head + B-roll: business owner refreshing bank app, no notification
  • Factory floor with raw materials NOT being loaded (cuts to empty crates)
  • Calendar animation: days slipping by, weekend shaded
  • Quick screen recording: "Sent" status vs no incoming on supplier side
  • End card: a closed container on a truck driving away (the missed shipment)

2. Three operators, same wire, three amounts

Math P1-A14-1 · PH2 Final VO 60 seconds 157 words
Final VO (read this verbatim)

Here's what every business owner needs to know about how wire rate margin compounds across a year. Three business owners sent 50K to the same Chinese supplier on the same day. Supplier received three different amounts. 49,250. 49,700. 49,850. A 600 dollar spread on identical wires. Now compound it. 12 wires a year at 50K each. 600 thousand dollars in sourcing volume. The business owner who used the bank lost around 9,000 dollars. The business owner who used the specialist lost around 1,800. That's a 7,200 dollar gap in one year. On the same business. Run it across three years. 21,600 dollars. That's an employee. That's a container of inventory. That's the next product line. And it's not a fee. It's the exchange rate margin. Banks bake 1 to 2% into the rate so you never see the line item. WorldFirst sits closer to 0.3% on the same wires. Same day to Chinese bank accounts. Search WorldFirst.

Angle

Same wire, same supplier, same day, three different amounts received. The variation IS the lesson. Then compound it across a year and three years.

Why the hook works

"Three operators, same wire, three amounts" — promise of a clean factual reveal. Audience leans in for the numbers.

Story beats (in order)
  1. Setup (3 owners, same supplier, same day, same 50K wire)
  2. Reveal (49,250 / 49,700 / 49,850 — 600 dollar spread)
  3. Annual compounding (12 wires/year, 600K volume)
  4. Per-operator loss (9K bank vs 1.8K specialist)
  5. Three-year math (21,600 dollar gap)
  6. Reframe ("That's an employee. That's a container. That's the next product line.")
  7. Mechanism ("Not a fee. It's the exchange rate margin. Banks bake 1 to 2% in.")
  8. WF positioning (0.3% on same wires, same-day to Chinese banks)
Numbers to nail (deliver exactly)

49,250 / 49,700 / 49,850 · 600 · 50K · 12 wires · 600,000 volume · 9,000 loss · 1,800 loss · 7,200 gap · 21,600 over 3 years · 1-2% bank margin · 0.3% WF margin

Tone & pacing

Confident, mathematical, slightly intense. Hormozi PAS energy. Each number lands like a punch.

Visual / B-roll ideas (director's call, these are starting points)
  • Three split-screen wire receipts showing the three different received amounts
  • Calculator on-screen as the math unfolds (12 × 50K, multiplied by margin)
  • Stacks of bills growing as the 3-year loss builds to 21,600
  • Cutaway at the "employee / container / product line" line: job posting, container photo, product launch
  • Final beat: a clean comparison bar chart — bank margin vs WF margin

3. Find the hidden FX margin in 30 seconds

Demo P2-A8-1 · PH2 Final VO 30 seconds (matches the "30 seconds" promise in the hook) 143 words
Final VO (read this verbatim)

Can a business owner find a hidden foreign exchange fee in 30 seconds? Watch. Watch. Step one. Pull up your last wire confirmation. Five seconds. Find the field that says applied rate or exchange rate used. Got it. Step two. Open a new tab. Google USD to the local currency you wired to. Look at the rate. That's the mid-market rate. Five seconds. Step three. Compare. Your bank's rate versus Google's rate. The gap is the margin. Twenty seconds in, you've found it. Step four. Multiply the gap by the wire amount. Ten seconds. That's the fee. No line item. No receipt. Just hidden inside the rate. Most business owners wire 50K to 100K per order. A 2 percent gap on a 50K wire is 1,000 dollars. Per wire. WorldFirst gives you a rate near Google's mid-market. The hidden fee disappears. Search WorldFirst.

Angle

Interactive challenge. The viewer can do this WITH the talent in real time. 30-second hands-on tutorial = proof of value via demonstration.

Why the hook works

"Can a business owner find a hidden FX fee in 30 seconds? Watch." — challenge framing + tutorial promise. Forces engagement.

Story beats (in order)
  1. Challenge frame ("30 seconds, watch")
  2. Step 1 (5s — pull up wire confirmation, find "applied rate")
  3. Step 2 (10s — Google "USD to local currency", note the mid-market rate)
  4. Step 3 (compare — the gap is the hidden margin)
  5. Step 4 (multiply — gap × wire amount = the hidden fee in dollars)
  6. Reveal ("No line item. No receipt. Just hidden inside the rate.")
  7. Scale ("2% gap on a 50K wire = 1,000 dollars. Per wire.")
  8. WF positioning (near Google's mid-market = hidden fee disappears)
Numbers to nail (deliver exactly)

30 seconds total · 5s/10s/20s/10s breakdown · 50K-100K wire range · 2% gap · 1,000 dollars per wire

Tone & pacing

Tutorial-friendly. Slightly conspiratorial ("they don't want you to know"). Hands-on, direct.

Visual / B-roll ideas (director's call, these are starting points)
  • Stopwatch overlay through all 30 seconds, ticking
  • Screen recording: anonymized bank wire confirmation, "applied rate" highlighted
  • Cut to Google search: "USD to MYR" (or THB, depending on cut region) — mid-market rate visible
  • Side-by-side comparison: bank rate vs Google rate, the gap highlighted
  • Calculator showing gap × 50,000 = the hidden fee number
  • Final beat: bank receipt with the hidden fee circled in red
